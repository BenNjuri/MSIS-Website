Science Fair Showcases Next-Generation Thinkers

Published: December 2025

← Back to News

Our main assembly hall was transformed into a bustling hub of innovation last week for the Annual Mustard Seed Science and Engineering Fair. Over 50 student-led projects were on display, tackling real-world challenges in health, agriculture, sustainability, and technology.

Addressing Real-World Problems

Students from both Junior and Senior School presented their findings to a panel of judges, which included our expert science faculty and visiting professors from local universities. The depth of research and creativity on show was truly astounding.

Standout projects included a fully functional prototype for a low-cost water purification system, an investigation into the antimicrobial properties of indigenous Kenyan plants, and a series of student-coded AI models designed to optimize local traffic flow.

The Scientific Method in Action

Beyond the impressive final products, the most encouraging aspect of the fair was observing the rigorous application of the scientific method. Our students were confident in explaining their hypotheses, their experimental designs, and how they accounted for variables and potential errors.

Inspiring Future Innovators

Events like the Science Fair are a critical component of our Cambridge curriculum, designed to cultivate informed curiosity and independent research skills. We are so proud of all the students who participated and we are certain we are looking at the next generation of engineers, doctors, and tech leaders.
